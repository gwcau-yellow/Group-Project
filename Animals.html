Affect on Animals

"Over 1 million seabirds and 100,000 sea mammals are killed by pollution every year. Immediately after the Exxon Valdez oil spill,more than 100,000 sea birds died, along with more than 1,000 sea otters. 144 bald eagles also died as a result of the spill. Air pollutants called chloroflurocarbons, or CFC's for short are thinning the Ozone layer, exposing animals, plants and other organisms to the suns ultra violet radiation.\ " Source
